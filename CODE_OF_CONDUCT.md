# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

The UCT Department of Electrical Engineering GitHub organisation is a shared working space for staff, postgraduate students and collaborators. We are committed to making participation a respectful and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity or level of experience.

This code applies alongside, and never in place of, the University of Cape Town's own policies on conduct, harassment and discrimination. Where this code and a UCT policy overlap, the UCT policy takes precedence.

## Expected behaviour

- Treat others with respect and good faith.
- Give and accept constructive feedback graciously.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Credit others' work and cite sources appropriately.
- Take responsibility for mistakes and learn from them.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation or discrimination of any kind.
- Personal or derogatory comments, or sustained disruption of others' work.
- Publishing another person's private information without their permission.
- Plagiarism, or misrepresenting the work of others as your own.
- Any conduct that would breach UCT policy or South African law.

## Scope

This code applies within all organisation spaces, including repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions and any associated communication, and whenever an individual is representing the organisation or the department.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, please report it. You can raise it with:

- Your supervisor, the course convener or the Head of Department.
- An organisation administrator, listed on the [organisation profile](https://github.com/uct-eee-department).
- The relevant UCT office for the formal handling of harassment or discrimination.

Reports will be handled as discreetly as the circumstances allow. Serious matters will be referred to UCT's formal processes, which govern any disciplinary outcome.

## Enforcement

Administrators may take any action they consider appropriate within the organisation, including editing or removing content and adjusting or removing access. Matters that fall under UCT policy or South African law will be referred to the appropriate university structures, which hold authority over any disciplinary action.

## Attribution

This code of conduct follows conventions common to open-source projects, adapted for a university department. It defers in all cases to the official policies of the University of Cape Town.
